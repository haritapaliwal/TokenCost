# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Strategy

## Target User (Specific)

**Primary Persona: "The Accountable Engineering Manager"**

> Engineering manager at a Series A SaaS startup with 5–15 developers, currently spending $500–$2,000/month on AI tools (Cursor, Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT), who just received a Slack message from their CFO asking: _"Can you break down what we're paying for all these AI subscriptions?"_

**Key characteristics:**

- Manages 5–15 devs, owns the tooling budget
- Approved individual AI subscriptions reactively, never audited the stack
- Feels mild guilt about not knowing exactly what each person uses
- Trusts data over opinions — will act on a clear savings number
- Slightly afraid of being the person who "wasted" $800/mo on duplicate tools

**Secondary Persona: "The Frugal Solo Founder"**

> Solo or 2-person technical founder on a $5K/mo runway, paying for 3–5 AI tools, unsure if they actually need all of them, and looking for any way to cut burn.

## First 100 Users in 30 Days ($0 Budget)

### Week 1: Launch Posts

1. **Post on Indie Hackers "Show IH"** thread with audit result screenshot + shareable link
2. **Submit to Hacker News "Show HN"** — title: _"Show HN: I built a free 60-second audit to find waste in your AI tool stack"_
3. **Post in r/SaaS** and **r/ExperiencedDevs** — lead with the dollar number, not the product

### Week 2: Direct Outreach

4. **DM 20 YC founders** whose companies appear in AI spending discussions on X
5. **DM 10 devs** who have tweeted about Cursor, Copilot, or Claude pricing in the past 30 days
6. **Post the shareable audit link** in 5 Discord/Slack communities with a real result (your own audit)

### Week 3: Content Loop

7. **Tweet a thread**: _"I audited 10 startup AI stacks. Here's what I found..."_ — link to tool
8. **Respond to every tweet** mentioning "AI tool costs", "Cursor pricing", "Copilot vs Claude" with the tool link
9. **Post one case study** (anonymized): Tool → Savings → Action taken

### Week 4: Compounding

10. **Ask early users to share their audit link** — the shareable URL is the viral mechanism
11. **Target Product Hunt** launch (Thursday)
12. **Email captured leads** with a "refer a friend" message

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

**Credex's existing customer base IS the target audience.**

Credex already sells AI credits to startups and developers. Every existing Credex customer:

- Is already spending money on AI tools ✅
- Already trusts Credex with their payment details ✅
- Is exactly the person this audit is built for ✅

**Action:** Send a single email to the Credex customer list:

> _"We built a free tool to tell you if you're overpaying for AI subscriptions. Takes 60 seconds. Here's the link."_

This is the one channel no competitor can replicate.
